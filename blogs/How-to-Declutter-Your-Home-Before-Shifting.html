How to Declutter Your Home Before Shifting

March 24, 2023

Moving for different reasons is brilliant, but have you considered organizing your home before moving? Not only can decluttering help you to cut down your possessions and save space, but it also helps you save money and time. The most important thing to remember is that the more important stuff you accumulate, the more you pay to move it. So, take a close look to discover more than what you require. Are you looking for clever ways to get rid of clutter before moving? You're in luck! You're in the right spot! We've compiled a list of professional strategies to help you declutter your process to help you start from scratch in your new home.

Room-To-Room Decluttering Checklist

Decluttering a house can be daunting and requires professional help to make the process easier. Our checklist can help you eliminate your stress points to finish the task of downsizing. Let's get started!

Living Room

As the decluttering process suggests, knowing where and where to begin is crucial. Living rooms are living space that is shared and has items that showcase your home's appeal. To begin, look through these spaces for things that are old, broken, damaged, or unnecessary. This brings peace, and your move day will be more relaxing. If you clean out your family and living spaces before moving, ensure that you take note of the following points:

  • Books and Magazines

    There are many effective methods to store and use books you want to read. You can purchase a custom bookcase to arrange categories and much more. But books aren't light objects to move, carry, or store in smaller boxes. Therefore, don't donate books that aren't relevant to your interests.

  • Game Consoles and Extra Controllers

    Take some time to play with the gamer in you. Sell the games you've played, get rid of the broken controllers, and add extra cables to conserve space and reduce the amount of work.

  • Old Cassettes and CDs

    With the rise of streaming platforms like Netflix, Spotify, Prime Video and Hotstar, There could be other reasons to collect CDs for DVDs. Give your old media to libraries or your family and friends. Posting your objects on amazon to make a real profit is also worthwhile.

Kitchen

Get rid of anything broken, excess, spoiled or anything that has never been used.

  • Use the Consumables First

    Utilizing consumables is an easy way to carry less stuff during the moving process. For instance, if you invest in something other than packaging materials for wrapping them. This can help reduce costs and free up space for other things in the bag. You can also eat as many pantry items as possible to have less food to carry around. This way, you will be able to protect food items from contamination. You can use the same method for personal hygiene products such as shampoo, soap, and wash lotion. Therefore, this is a proactive approach.

  • Kitchen Appliances

    When appliances are taken out of warranty and wear and tear, they must be replaced. Therefore, you should dispose of appliances that no longer serve a purpose. You can also sell old corroded machines or exchange them and then transport them directly to the new location. Recycling is eco-friendly, which includes appliances like microwaves, toasters, grills, and toasters.

  • Cookware

    The majority of kitchenware is comprised of baking sheets and cookie dishes. Pizza stones you don't use or the tops for pots that aren't matching are additional items to search for and give away.

Bedrooms

Bedrooms are among the most effective places to get rid of clutter. Before moving, clean. Because of the sheer volume of belongings, closets and dressers become storage spaces that are difficult to access. At least once per year, it's an excellent idea to go through our belongings and get rid of things we no longer require or do not want. Look for items like the ones below:

  • Clothes Hangers

    Who has only hangers that they can use? Donating hangers to family relatives, trusts for charitable purposes, or second-hand shops is a great idea.

  • Clothes and Shoes

    Time to think about your body's goals. Consider the number of clothes that are suitable for you. Please don't throw away tight dresses and clothes that have spent most of your lives in the closet. Find a recycling facility for textiles near. Also, those shoes and clothes might not be beneficial to your needs. However, the situation is different for other people. You can donate them to orphanages for a fair share of the charity.

  • Jewellery and Accessories

    Since accessories and jewellery are rapidly becoming out of fashion, There is plenty of space for decluttering. Keep the jewellery you cherish; however, think about donating things you aren't wearing to make a person's day more enjoyable. You can also take bags, belts, or jewellery pieces to a consignment store.

How Will Your New House Influence Your Needs

When you begin decluttering before moving, consider the space needed to store your possessions when you move into your new house. You'll have to get rid of furniture you don't want due to the size of your bedrooms and the overall arrangement of your new home. Consider what you could incorporate into your new space, where it will be, and other aspects.
